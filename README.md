## Overview
This repository hosts the interactive research paper and supplementary materials for the study on visual perceptual completion. The human visual system constantly encounters fragmented sensory inputs due to environmental occlusions and anatomical deficits. Despite this, perception remains phenomenologically continuous. This research investigates the neurological, developmental, and evolutionary architectures underlying this phenomenon, exploring the brain's capacity to synthesize complete object representations from partial (or "half-seen") stimuli.  

## Key Concepts Explored
* **Amodal & Modal Completion:** The brain's capacity to interpolate missing information and synthesize unperceived features, creating boundaries and surfaces that do not physically exist.  

* **Predictive Coding:** The framework detailing how the brain acts as a Bayesian inference engine. It constantly generates internal mental models to predict sensory inputs and suppresses predictable signals to maximize metabolic efficiency.  

* **One-Shot Perceptual Learning:** The extraordinary ability of the human brain to irreversibly alter its perceptual architecture after a single momentary visual exposure, a process heavily reliant on priors stored in the high-level visual cortex (HLVC).

* **Evolutionary Ecology:** Amodal completion serves as a hyper-sensitive bias engineered by natural selection to rapidly identify threats (e.g., hidden predators) from partial visual data, which in turn drives the evolutionary arms race of animal camouflage.

## Interactive Application Features
The included index.html file allows users to physically engage with the psychological concepts before analyzing the empirical data:

1. **Illusion Generator:** Demonstrates modal/amodal completion by letting users align disjointed elements to trigger the brain's automatic rendering of an illusory shape.

2. **Recognition Thresholds:** Plots object recognition accuracy against visibility percentages, illustrating the non-linear cognitive leap that occurs when approximately 30-50% of an object is visible.

3. **Neural Activation Data:** Compares fMRI BOLD signal intensities between the Early Visual Cortex (V1) and the Lateral Occipital Complex (LOC) during the perception of occluded objects.

## Conclusion
The phenomenon of perceptual completion bridges the gap between raw sensory data and conscious phenomenological experience. As demonstrated in this research, the human need to visually process only a fraction of a scene is not a cognitive error, but a highly sophisticated, survival-optimized adaptation of the nervous system. The brain's ability to "create the other half" relies on an intricate cortical hierarchy where feedforward structural heuristics interface dynamically with massive top-down predictive priors stored in the high-level visual cortex.  

The discovery of one-shot perceptual learning further underscores the profound cognitive penetrability of vision, revealing a system optimized for rapid inference rather than passive, objective recording. Ultimately, human perception is fundamentally an act of controlled neural hallucination, guided by sensory input but authored by evolutionary expectation and prior knowledge. As artificial intelligence increasingly adopts biologically inspired feedback loops, decoding these predictive algorithms will be vital for bridging the gap between biological vision and machine learning.
